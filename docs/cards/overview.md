# AI Application Cards overview

Microsoft's Application and Platform Cards are intended to help you understand how our AI technology works, the choices application owners can make that influence application performance and behavior, and the importance of considering the whole application, including the technology, the people, and the environment.

Application cards are created for AI applications and platform cards are created for AI platform services. These resources can support the development or deployment of your own applications and can be shared with users or stakeholders impacted by them.

## What's in an Application Card?

Each Application Card provides comprehensive information about an AI-powered Windows application, including:

- **Overview** - What the application does and how AI is integrated
- **Key features** - The AI capabilities available in the application
- **Intended uses** - Scenarios and industries where the application is designed to be used
- **Models and training data** - Information about the AI models powering the features
- **Performance** - How the application performs under various conditions
- **Limitations** - Important boundaries and constraints to consider
- **Evaluations** - How the application has been tested for quality and safety
- **Safety components** - Built-in protections and mitigations
- **Best practices** - Guidance for deploying and using the application responsibly
- **Learn more** - Additional resources and documentation

## Responsible AI principles

As part of its commitment to responsible AI, Microsoft adheres to six core principles: fairness, reliability and safety, privacy and security, inclusiveness, transparency, and accountability. These principles are embedded in the Responsible AI Standard, which guides teams in designing, building, and testing AI applications.

Application and Platform Cards play a key role in operationalizing these principles by offering transparency around capabilities, intended uses, and limitations. For further insight, explore Microsoft's [Responsible AI Transparency Report](https://www.microsoft.com/ai/responsible-ai) and Code of Conduct, which outline how enterprise customers and consumers can engage with AI responsibly.
